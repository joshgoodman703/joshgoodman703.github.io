Seinfeld, the Show About Nothing

I would love to introduce you to the world of Seinfeld, a series of misadventures with Jerry Seinfeld, George Costanza, Elaine Bennis, and Cosmo Kramer. This show, directed and produced by Jerry Seinfeld and Larry David, set the precedent for the modern situational comedy (sitcom). When it debuted in 1989, it was not expected to be the massive hit that it became. The show has a very simple concept - "nothing" happens. Of course, that does not mean that we are following a series of people sitting and twiddling their thumbs, rather we are not expecting one singular plot to drive the entire show. It was uncommon at the time for a show to be based purely on the everyday happenings of individuals. For that reason, Seinfeld became popular simply based on the idea that there was not a significant or overarching storyline that drove each episode. Instead, each episode had its own storyline or set of storylines that drove the plot. By next week, everything from the prior episode is cleared up, and there is a new set of events that transpire. This truly was the jumping off point for the modern sitcom, making Jerry Seinfeld and Larry David regarded as the first movers and architects of this space.
